An Introduction to Mental Health


Mental Illnesses are mostly termed as disorders but, most of you will ask “What is a DISORDER??” Well a disorder is a break in the normal functionality which differs from case to case and maybe even unique in permutation and Combination. A confusion on the precise definition of disease is likely, so for you guys here’s an example. A disease is an issue that has the same signs arising. Like for fever it is higher temperature and for scurvy it is bleeding gums. Whereas in a disorder the case is way more complex. The reactions are different from person to person. Only patterns are found to be similar. To explain the difference between patterns and disease we can think of two cars with certain issues. All cars had an engine problem which was similar in nature hence, this is a disease. But a few of the cars had a problem with the door lock but the parts of the door affected were different. This is a pattern and hence is a disorder. What’s strange is that people often debate the existence of these disorders. Human behaviour is very sensitive to our state of being and can be split into two main types which are: Social Behaviour : People mostly like to be in a group like in high school or college it’s a notion that larger the group, cooler the kids. The loner is called an outcast, weirdo or even bullied in extreme cases. Being in a group helps a person get more yes meaning that their ego is satisfied. A person who says more yes or agrees to you more is perceived as friendly and the vice-versa is most often hated. Seeing is Believing : The most trusted hands are our own. In case we don’t get approval from people we turn to our most trusted bodily sense. The eyes. Now, as we talked about the above points let’s put it in context. Since eternity due to the fact that the brain has been a mystery, people have undermined the importance of the brain and termed it as aberrant or deviant. Now obviously a person who is behaving wildly differently is viewed down upon, as a result they are outcast of the society. And coming to a possibility it was counter intuitive due to the lack of advances in technology. What gives us pain is that even after nearly a century of study of the mind, Mental Health patients are still viewed as CRAZY, STUPID and even ignored! But let’s take a logical take on this: the heart, lung, liver and ovaries all are organs right? Issues like Ovarian cancer, Jaundice, and high amounts of bad Cholesterol (Low Density Lipid Protein) is dealt seriously, patients are taken care with utmost care. The Brain is essential to survival and the most important organ to a better life. Yet, when we come across a patient who suffers from mental health issues we tend to ignore them? How does this make any sense? Think about it my dear readers.
